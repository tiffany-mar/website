About Me

My name is Anika Sharma, a freshman at Dublin High School, and welcome to my Online Portfolio. My personal career goal? I want to be a software developer. When I learnt about software development, I did not immediately know it was exactly what I wanted to do with my life, but it always stuck with me. As I gained more experience and explored my other interests, I realized I loved the problem solving when debugging, the excitement of figuring out how to code a new idea, and the satisfaction when everything finally comes together. I am happily enrolled in the Engineering Academy. To get paid to build apps and websites would be amazing. To achieve this goal, I hope to gain more experience in other languages and to continue to build programs.

I started coding at a young age. When I first discovered programming, I spent the whole day getting a turtle to make stars and hearts through a series of commands in MIT Logo. Since then I have learnt a couple different programming languages and I continue to learn more in my Computer Science Principles class this year. The computer I used back then was a Dell Inspiron 8600 2004 laptop clocking in at a "modest" 7.6 pounds. Technology has been rapidly changing since then and it continues to do so. My experience in different languages has given me the ability to be able to pick up new languages easily. I am also a quick learner, which allows me to learn new concepts, processes and ideas quickly. This can help in the workplace when company uses a programming language that I have not learnt yet or has a different way of doing things.

Away from the computer screen, I was also involved in the school musical, The Pajama Game. I was part of tech crew, and our job was to make show come to life. My team and I would move set and props of all sizes on and off stage. The experience helped me learn how important communication is. During transitions, we would often not be able to see each other and we were definitely not allowed to speak. Therefore, as team, we would plan exactly who turned how much and how many steps they would take. Another key part of the job was staying organized. Throughout shows, tech crew would need to manage where props where, how they were placed, and making sure pathways were clear for set pieces and cast. I would also need to be independent, as everyone was very busy and no one would be able to help.

Last year, I took a difficult class. At the end of the year, I was recognized by my teacher in front of the school. She said I had grit. My experiences and interests lie in computing and working with others to accomplish a goal, but most importantly, I will persevere.

Thank you for your time and feel free to look through the rest of my portfolio.

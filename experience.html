Work Experience

As part of the Dublin High School Engineering and Design Academy, I have been introduced to careers and workplaces in the engineering field. I have learned a lot through field trips and numerous guest speakers. These experiences have helped me gain more insight into topics that I am already interested in or I want to explore more.

I was able to visit Lawrence Livermore National Laboratory's National Ignition Facility. The National Ignition Facility is a large laser facility built to research fusion. Our tour guide was a computer scientist who worked at the facility. She provided us with more knowledge of how the facility utilizes computer science and computer scientists, which is the field that I am more interested in. This tour was also very engaging because it related to my interests in the future of energy and alternative energy.

Dr. Jason Yosinski was an influential guest speaker. He is a machine learning scientist and a founding member of Uber AI Labs. Dr. Yosinski talked to our class about machine learning and he demonstrated a couple of his projects, such as a deep visualization toolbox. I am very interested in machine learning and I had just started learning machine learning outside of class when Dr. Yosinski spoke in our class. Dr. Yosinski provided me with a deeper explanation of what neural networks are and how they work. While he was completing his Ph.D. at Cornell, Dr. Yosinski worked on a quadruped robot that learned to move. I really enjoyed learning about this project and this inspired me to want to learn more about artificial intelligence.
